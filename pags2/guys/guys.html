Types of Security

  • Active and Passive Security
  • Physical and Logical Security
  • Personal and Information Systems Security

Active and Passive Security

Active security measures involve proactive actions taken to prevent security breaches, while passive security measures are designed to minimize damage after a breach has occurred.

  • Active Security: Measures that actively prevent security incidents, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and regular security audits.
  • Passive Security: Measures that reduce the impact of security incidents, including data backups, disaster recovery plans, and incident response strategies.

Physical and Logical Security

Physical security refers to the protection of physical assets, while logical security involves protecting digital assets through software solutions.

  • Physical Security: Protection of physical assets such as buildings, equipment, and personnel through locks, surveillance cameras, and security guards.
  • Logical Security: Protection of digital assets through software solutions, including encryption, access controls, and secure coding practices.

Personal and Information Systems Security

Personal security focuses on protecting individuals from threats, while information systems security aims to protect organizational data and IT infrastructure.

  • Personal Security: Measures to protect individuals from threats such as identity theft and personal attacks, including personal safety training and awareness programs.
  • Information Systems Security: Protection of organizational data and IT infrastructure from unauthorized access, breaches, and cyber threats through comprehensive security policies and technologies.
